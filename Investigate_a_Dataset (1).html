Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

This data set contains information about 10,000 movies collected from The Movie Database (TMDb), including user ratings and revenue.

The columns in this dataset are the following:

['id', 'imdb_id', 'popularity', 'budget', 'revenue', 'original_title', 'cast', 'homepage', 'director', 'tagline', 'keywords', 'overview', 'runtime', 'genres', 'production_companies', 'release_date', 'vote_count', 'vote_average', 'release_year', 'budget_adj', 'revenue_adj']

The most importants columns that will be considered in the analysis are the following:

['director','runtime', 'release_year','revenue_adj']

Question(s) for Analysis¶

The questions for analysis are the following:

. What are the top 5 directors in terms of revenue?

. What kinds of properties are associated with movies that have higher revenues?

Conclusions¶

The mains findings are the following:

. The directors that generated the greatest amount of adjusted revenue are Steven Spielberg, James Cameron, Peter Jackson, George Lucas and Robert Zemeckis. Steven Spielberg, generated an adjusted revenue that is considerably higher than the one generated by the other directors.

. As movies get longer, the adjusted revenue generated increases. Also, movies that are very long have an adjusted revenue that is significantly higher than the rest.

. Movies that are very old have the highest adjusted revenue. However, movies that are very old, moderately old and moderately new have a similar adjusted revenue. On the other hand, movies that are very new have a significantly lower adjusted revenue. It is important to consider that older movies have the advantage of having more time in which revenue can accumulate. This is probably why movies that are very new have a significantly lower amount of adjusted revenue. It would be better to consider adjusted revenue generated on the year of release to make a better analysis.
